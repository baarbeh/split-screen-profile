Jeremy Baarbé

B.A. (Hons), M.A., JD Candidate, 2018

I am a University of Ottawa Common Law student interested in intellectual property, innovation, and information technology law. I am searching for articling opportunities starting Summer, 2018.

I use technology to provide insight to challenging legal questions. Working closely with Professor Jeremy de Beer and the Open African Innovation Research Network, my recent projects include tracking the adoption of IP Treaties across Africa and studying how judicial review contributes to delays at the Copyright Board. In 2016, I assisted Professor de Beer intervene before the Supreme Court on the utility standard in patent law in Astrazeneca v Apotex. In Summer, 2017 I am conducting field research in Nairobi, Kenya to see how high tech firms are using IP and open data to innovate.

I have experience leading successful teams. Most recently, I led the University of Ottawa at the Harold G. Fox Intellectual Property Moot in February, 2017 where we won the Fox Cup for best team and the Gordon F. Henderson Award for best factum.

My diverse skills include research, web development, graphic design, marketing, statistical analysis, and tutoring.
