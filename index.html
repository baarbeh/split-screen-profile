Jeremy Baarbé
JD Candidate, 2018


I am a University of Ottawa Common Law student interested in intellectual property, innovation, and information technology law. During my Masters of Arts in Criminology, and throughout my studies, I have focused on using technology and data analyses to answer challenging legal questions.

I have extensive legal research experience from working closely with Professor Jeremy de Beer and the Open African Innovation Research Network (Open AIR). As a New and Emerging Researcher, my research includes tracking the adoption of IP Treaties across Africa, licencing of open data for Agriculture, and a study of how Judicial Review contributes to delays at the Copyright Board. As part of Open AIR, I am planning field research in Nairobi, Kenya in Summer, 2017 to study how high tech fims there are using IP and open data to innovate.

An entrepreneurial self-starter, I have experience leading successful teams. Most recently, I led a team from University of Ottawa to compete at the Harold G. Fox Intellectual Property Moot in February, 2017 where we won the Cup for best team and the Gordon F. Henderson Award for best factum.

I am currently searching for articling opportunities starting in May, 2018.

Outside of law, my skills include web development, graphic design, marketing, statistical analysis, and tutoring. I would love to discuss how I can help you communicate with excellence. I look forward to connecting!
