JD Candidate, 2018


I am a University of Ottawa Common Law student interested in intellectual property, innovation, and information technology law. Throughout my graduate and legal studies, I have focused on using technology to provide insight to legal questions. I am currently searching for articling opportunities starting Summer, 2018.

I work closely with Professor Jeremy de Beer and the Open African Innovation Research Network (Open AIR). As a New and Emerging Researcher, my work includes tracking the adoption of IP Treaties across Africa, exploring the licencing of open data for agriculture, and studying how judicial review contributes to delays at the Copyright Board. As part of Open AIR, I am conducting field research during Summer, 2017 in Nairobi, Kenya to study how high tech fims there are using IP and open data to innovate.

An entrepreneurial self-starter, I have experience leading successful teams. Most recently, I led the University of Ottawa at the Harold G. Fox Intellectual Property Moot in February, 2017 where we won the Cup for best team and the Gordon F. Henderson Award for best factum.

I have a diverse skill set, including web development, graphic design, marketing, statistical analysis, and tutoring. When I'm not working I enjoy cycling, photography, and building LED light art. I look forward to connecting!
